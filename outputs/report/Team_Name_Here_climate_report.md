## Our Climate Question

How has drought frequency changed on Pine Ridge over the past 30 years, and which communities are most exposed?

---

## Key Findings

**Watershed Health Score: 71 / 100**

- Drought Index: 53.6/100 (weight: 54%)
- Streamflow Reliability: 91.9/100 (weight: 46%)

---

## Top Three Concerns

1. Increasing drought frequency and severity, particularly since 2010
2. Declining low flows on the White River, with more days below emergency thresholds
3. Insufficient water monitoring infrastructure on the reservation — data gaps are a policy gap

## Top Three Opportunities

1. Establish Tribal water monitoring stations at key points on the White River system
2. Develop a Tribal drought early warning system using PDSI and real-time gauge data
3. Integrate traditional ecological knowledge with earth observation data for more complete picture

## Additional Data Needed

- Groundwater monitoring wells across the Arikaree aquifer under Pine Ridge
- Community-collected water quality and streamflow data (Tribal-owned, OCAP-governed)
- High-resolution land cover and vegetation change data at the field scale

## Data Governance

This analysis used publicly available federal datasets:
- NOAA Palmer Drought Severity Index (climate divisions 7–8, South Dakota)
- USGS NWIS streamflow (White River gauges)
- Daymet daily climate (NASA/ORNL)
- Census TIGER AIANNH boundaries

These datasets describe the land and waters of the Oglala Lakota people.
Under OCAP® principles, the Oglala Lakota Nation retains the right to
Ownership, Control, Access, and Possession of data about their lands.
This analysis should be reviewed by and shared with the appropriate
Tribal departments before any public release.

Governance frameworks applied: OCAP® | CARE | FAIR | IEEE 2890-2025
